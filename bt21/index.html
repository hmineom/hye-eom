BT21 × iOttie — Licensed character collaboration · 2025
The Challenge

Licensed character products tend to one of two failures: too literal (a recognizable character printed onto a generic object) or too cautious, where the IP becomes invisible. The brief asked for a BT21 product that fans would recognize as BT21, and that anyone else would want as a piece of industrial design first.

The Solution

Lead with the product. Let CMF and packaging carry the IP. A single refined silhouette across four colorways, each one tuned to a character's emotional register. The result reads as BT21 to fans and as a confident accessory line to everyone else without compromising either audience.

Concept

Characters carried by the fan, not the print.

BT21 is not one character, it's seven, each with a distinct fanbase. Rather than crowd the product with all of them, we included a sheet of DIY stickers featuring all seven characters, letting fans personalize the product themselves, claim their bias, mix favorites, or leave it clean.

The product itself stays neutral, which means it earns shelf space next to non-licensed accessories and invites buyers who like the form before they notice the IP. Fans get something unmistakably BT21 with a layer of play built in. Non-fans get a well-designed accessory with an optional personality.

Behind the Scenes

The work behind the work.

Three streams of coordination ran in parallel. Working directly with Line Friends USA, I aligned expectations from HYBE Entertainment and managed the approval flow — keeping revision cycles from eating the timeline.

On the manufacturing side, I led CN supplier coordination across multiple QC rounds, testing form, CMF finish consistency, and color matching at each pass across all SKUs. Beyond designing the lineup, I was heavily involved in team alignment, inventory planning, MSRP and cost calculations, and overall supply chain oversight, keeping every layer moving in sync.
