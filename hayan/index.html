HAYAN — Sustainable lifestyle brand · 2021
The Challenge

The eco-friendly toothbrush category had a ceiling. Bamboo handles, muted palettes, design that signaled virtue instead of desire. It never tried to appeal beyond the people who already cared. A higher price needed a reason to exist — without a brand to give it one, it would land as another apologetic eco product.

The Solution

We reframed the entry point. Instead of competing on eco credentials, HAYAN led with desire. Objects worth wanting that happened to be sustainable. That meant building a brand identity, CMF language, and packaging system that could make someone want the product before they knew it was sustainable.

Hand-stamped, deliberately imperfect. A mark that feels as raw and natural as the material behind it.

Product & CMF

Pistachio mint, not earth tones.

Eco products leaned earthy. HAYAN went the other direction. A pistachio mint that doesn't read as "eco." It reads as home. Handle angled toward the bristle head for easier reach. Bristle count increased from 1,000 to 2,500.

A toothbrush sits on the counter every day. Most users hide theirs. HAYAN was designed to be left out — something you'd be quietly proud to have on the counter.

Packaging

Bold message, refined material.

Printed large on uncoated kraft, I'm not plastic functions less as packaging copy and more as a brand position made physical. No imagery. No eco iconography. Confidence communicated through what was left out.

The typographic choice was precise: I'M and PLA pulled into color within the phrase, surfacing both the voice and the material in a single gesture.
