HAYAN

A sustainable lifestyle brand. Its first product is a biodegradable toothbrush — not because the brand is about toothbrushes, but because the toothbrush is one of the most overlooked objects in the daily ritual of waste.

The brief, and the reframe

They came for a toothbrush. I gave them a brand.

Econnect came to me to design a PLA toothbrush. I could have just designed the product. But PLA meant a higher price than bamboo alternatives already on shelf. A higher price needed a reason.

Without a brand to give it one, it would land as another eco-friendly product with the same apologetic vibes as everything else. So I proposed building a brand around a different idea: sustainability without compromise.

Econnect agreed. I came on as co-founder, leading creative direction end-to-end.

The problem

The category had a ceiling.

Bamboo handles, muted palettes, design that signaled virtue instead of desire. Eco-friendly toothbrushes never tried to appeal beyond the people who already cared.

The material

A bioplastic that earns its form.

PLA, a corn-based bioplastic, decomposes in 6–12 months vs 500+ years for conventional plastic. 70% less carbon in manufacturing. Higher elasticity, actually a functional advantage in toothbrush production. And critically, PLA doesn't absorb moisture the way bamboo does. Bamboo's mold problem, gone. The material solved the friction that made bamboo feel like a daily compromise.

Sustainable didn't mean easy to make. Early composite formulations couldn't withstand tooling temperatures and came out warped, brittle, or too flexible to hold their form. Getting the ratio right required iterative reformulation, and every adjustment shifted how the material absorbed pigment, making color consistency something that had to be actively monitored rather than assumed.

Product design

Form driven by experience.

Handle angled toward the bristle head for easier reach. Bristle count increased from 1,000 to 2,500. Children's version rounded to encourage lighter pressure.

CMF

Pistachio mint, not earth tones.

Eco products leaned earthy. HAYAN went the other direction. A pistachio mint that doesn't read as "eco." It reads as home.

A toothbrush sits on the counter every day. Most users hide theirs. HAYAN was designed to be left out. Something you'd be quietly proud to have on the counter.

Packaging

A single line of copy did the work of a campaign.

Printed large on uncoated kraft, I'm not plastic functions less as packaging copy and more as a brand position made physical. No imagery. No eco iconography. Confidence communicated through what was left out.

The typographic choice was precise: I'M and PLA pulled into color within the phrase, surfacing both the voice and the material in a single gesture.

The system

A product designed to come back.

QR code on packaging links to a return service. Used toothbrushes get mailed back. Subscription handles replenishment. The brand owns the full lifecycle.
